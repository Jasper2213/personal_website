Sierpinski triangle

Github page Wikipedia page

The Sierpinski triangle is a simple yet beautiful mathematical pattern.

It starts by drawing a triangle with a base of 1000 pixels. Then, it randomly places a dot inside the triangle. From there, it selects one of three corners randomly and calculates the middle between the placed dot and the chosen corner.

When the middle is calculated, a dot is placed at this point. From there, it once again selects a random corner and calculates the middle between these points. This process keeps repeating for as long as the program runs. After a while, the Sierpinski Triangle becomes clearly visible:
